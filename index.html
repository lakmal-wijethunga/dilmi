About Me

I am a dedicated microbiologist with a Ph.D. from the University of Science, where I focused on genomic analysis of emerging pathogens. My research combines traditional microbiology techniques with cutting-edge bioinformatics to understand bacterial evolution and antibiotic resistance mechanisms.

Throughout my academic journey, I've developed expertise in next-generation sequencing, microbial culturing, and computational biology. I'm particularly interested in translating genomic insights into practical applications for public health and infectious disease management.

My career goal is to bridge the gap between laboratory research and real-world implementation of microbial genomics in healthcare settings. I am seeking opportunities to collaborate with forward-thinking research teams and industry partners on projects that advance our understanding of microbial communities and their impact on human health.
